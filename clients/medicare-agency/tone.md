## For future Claude
Voice and compliance rules for the Medicare Agency, stated by the operator
2026-07-04. Overrides agency/SOUL.md for this client. Every piece of
senior-facing or prospect-facing copy (ads, landing pages, emails, scripts)
MUST pass the voice rules AND the compliance hard checklist below — no
exceptions. Medicare marketing is CMS-regulated; a violation is a business
risk, not a style issue.

## Voice
Warm, plain-English, respectful, patient, non-pushy, educational.

### Use phrases like
- "Understand your options."
- "See what may fit your situation."
- "No-pressure review."
- "Licensed help with Medicare questions."
- "Plan availability and benefits depend on your area and eligibility."

### Never use
- "Best plan."
- "Guaranteed savings."
- "Free benefits."
- "Get everything you're entitled to."
- "Government-approved."
- "Medicare will cover this for you."
- "Act now or lose benefits."
- Anything implying affiliation with Medicare, CMS, Social Security, or the
  federal government unless literally true.

## Compliance hard checklist (every public-facing draft)
- [ ] Follows CMS Medicare marketing/communications rules (MA, MA-PD, PDP,
      cost plans) (as of 2026-07, CMS Medicare marketing guidelines)
- [ ] Marketing materials that require submission/review before distribution
      have gone through that review (as of 2026-07, federal marketing rules)
- [ ] One-to-one consent obtained — transparent and prominently placed —
      before sharing beneficiary information with TPMOs for
      marketing/enrollment contact (as of CY2025 CMS rule)
- [ ] No misleading benefit claims; no unsupported superlatives
- [ ] No pressure tactics or artificial urgency
- [ ] Proper disclaimers present
- [ ] Plan-specific details discussed only by licensed/certified agents
- [ ] No implied government affiliation
